31 Days of Kindness (Day 4) - 2015 Edition

in 31DaysOfKindness2015  

Welcome to Day 4 of #31DaysOfKindness, 2015 edition!

While the theme for today is to share an article with someone, I’m going to take inspiration from Ryan Avery and share ALL the articles from one of my favorite blogs with you - The Big Guy Blog on Runner’s World as written by Ted Spiker.

Why the entire blog and all of its articles? Read on and find out…

On April 18, 2012, Ted wrote:

I’ll start a virtual group for us to help push each other (you’re also welcome to join if you’re already well under 30 and just want to help push us along). In addition, I’ll also pick five of you, feature your stories here, and post your photo when you go under 30. We’re going to do this together. Digitally, but still together. ( Please, oh please, let there be five of us.)

We may not win the race, but we’re going to win ours.

This begat what has become the Sub-30 Club on Facebook…an international collection of runners (amateur and professional) who have come together to set and defeat goals, and to provide support for those who are working on attaining them.

A few years ago, I somehow stumbled into this group (when it was still fairly small) and started to read Ted’s blog. While humor does enter his articles, the ones that focus on achieving goals, restarting (which I did myself earlier in the year), and rebuilding are the ones I’ve found to be the most valuable.

Another benefit of this group (and the blog) is that I’ve found a bunch of like-minded individuals who have pushed me (and have accepted my pushing back), many of whom have become close friends. Some of them were even written about or quoted (including yours truly) in some of Ted’s articles.

I can’t narrow it down to one article, but Ted’s writing, leadership, and being able to bring together all walks of life under one common activity have been incredibly inspiring.

Mark Remy, me, and Ted Spiker at the 2014 Runner’s World Half & Festival in Bethlehem, PA
