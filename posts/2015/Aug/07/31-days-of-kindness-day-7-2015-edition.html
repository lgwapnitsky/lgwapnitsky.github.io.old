31 Days of Kindness (Day 7) - 2015 Edition

in 31DaysOfKindness2015  

Day 7 of #31DaysOfKindness, 2015 edition, allowed me to pick an act of kindness of my choice. With so many things to do (and so much time to do them today), what did I choose?

I jumped ahead in the game a bit today, but it was well-deserved.

Give thanks to one community servant you don’t personally know

Here’s the story. I went running at the local YMCA this afternoon. When I finished up, I went outside to sit on the bench in front of the building to cool down and upload my running data. Sitting on the bench next to me was a young boy who looked like he was waiting for someone, but he was also looking rather upset.

One of the staff of the Y came out and started talking to him. It turned out he had been dropped off by his father a few hours earlier, but nobody let the staff know. They assumed he was part of the camp program (which he wasn’t). His father said he was going to pick him up at 5 (it was 4:15), so he was just sitting outside, alone, waiting.

The staff member calmly talked to the boy and offered to let him go to the Kids’ Zone (her description of it being like a McDonald’s Playland, but bigger, was awesome). The staff took responsibility for this boy in the Kids’ Zone until his dad showed up.

Before picking up my daughter (after running a quick errand down the block), I checked back in with the staff member. The father had not yet picked up the boy (it was a little after 5:00 at this time). I commended her on how she treated the situation, and made the boy feel better about being able to wait and play instead of sitting alone on a bench

It’s people like that in the community (and at our local Y), who really make a big difference. I didn’t get the young woman’s name, but thank you so much for looking after the children of the community.
